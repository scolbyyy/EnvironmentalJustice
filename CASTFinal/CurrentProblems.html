What are current environmental justice problems?

Click on each issue to learn more.

This is not meant to be an exhaustive list, but a sampling which can inspire you to evaluate your own community.

Redlined Heat Islands

Fighting the phenomenon wherein Black neighborhoods become hotter in the summer than white neighborhoods, leading to serious heat-related health issues. Advocating for green, shaded spaces in everyone's neighborhoods while fighting gentrification.

Clean Drinking Water

Ensuring everyone has safe, clean water to drink.

Empowering Indigenous Peoples

When indigenous peoples are legally allowed to protect and manage their environments, the land tends to be used more sustainably. Nobody knows what the land needs like the people who have lived off of it for hundreds of years.

Homes Free From Toxins

Testing and removing lead paint from all homes, especially including lower-class homes and the homes of people of color, which disproportionately are exposed to the toxin.

Sustainable and Healthy Agriculture

Changing our current model of tilling, monoculture, pesticide based agriculture which drains nutrients from soils, causes erosion, and harms field workers.

Renewable Energy Transition

Replacing fossil fuels with clean sources of energy such as solar and wind. Providing safe jobs with livable wages for former fossil fuel workers.

Public Transportation

Strengthening and streamlining public transportation systems like buses, subways, and trains to make sure everybody can get where they need to go, not just people who can afford cars. Public transportation links vulnerable people into the resources they need and saves the planet in doing so.

Communities Free of Nuclear Waste

Making sure extremely hazardous waste is not produced excessively or deposited near where people live.
