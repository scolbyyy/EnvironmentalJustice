Where can I connect with activists?

Click on each issue to learn more.

This is not meant to be an exhaustive list, but a sampling which can inspire you to evaluate your own community. All the groups listed are present all over the United States. Some probably have a chapter near youâ€”or resources for you to start your own chapter.

Extinction Rebellion

Direct action on climate change, especially protests/demonstrations.

Natural Resources Defense Council (NDRC)

An environmental protection lobbying group. If you want suggestions about what to email/call your legislators about, this site will have some.

The Sunrise Movement

Organizing for the Green New Deal, a plan to solve the climate crisis and economic inequality by creating renewable energy jobs.

NAACP Climate Justice Resources

A lot of links the NAACP put together about climate justice, centered on Black experiences and activism. Of particular interest are the "toolkits," which provide ideas about actions you can take and advice about organizing.

Climate Justice Alliance

Empowers and organizes local communities around issues of importance to them, particularly focusing on agriculture/food and energy.

DSA Ecosocialists

A working group focused on the Green New Deal.

Mary Robinson Foundation

A climate justice foundation centered on women's experiences and activism.

We Act

A group which works to make sure low-income communities of color have a say in local enviromental legislation.
